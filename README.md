# SCT_DS_4 - Traffic Accident Analysis

##  Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing traffic accident data to identify patterns and factors associated with accident occurrence and severity. Through exploratory data analysis (EDA) and data visualization techniques, the project investigates the impact of weather conditions, road surface conditions, lighting conditions, speed limits, and area types on road accidents.

---

##  Objective

To analyze traffic accident data and identify patterns related to accident severity, weather conditions, road surface conditions, lighting conditions, and other contributing factors using data visualization and exploratory data analysis techniques.

##  Dataset

The dataset contains information related to traffic accidents, including:

* Accident Severity
* Weather Conditions
* Road Surface Conditions
* Light Conditions
* Speed Limit
* Urban or Rural Area
* Number of Casualties
* Number of Vehicles Involved
* Date and Time of Accident

### Dataset Source

The dataset used in this project is publicly available on Kaggle:

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/tsiaras/uk-road-safety-accidents-and-vehicles

**Note:** The original dataset is not included in this repository due to its large file size.

##  Key Insights

* Slight accidents constitute the majority of reported incidents.
* Weather conditions influence accident occurrence and severity.
* Lighting conditions impact road safety and accident frequency.
* Road surface conditions play an important role in accident patterns.
* Urban areas experience a higher concentration of accidents compared to rural regions.
* Most accidents involve a relatively small number of casualties.
* Accident severity varies across different weather conditions.

---

##  Conclusion

The analysis highlights the importance of environmental and road-related factors in traffic accident occurrence and severity. Understanding these patterns can help improve road safety strategies, driver awareness, and transportation planning.
